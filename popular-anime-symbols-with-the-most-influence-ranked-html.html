Popular Anime Symbols With The Most Influence

September 14, 2024
blog

Nazarick, the enigmatic dungeon ruled by Ainz Ooal Gown, takes the 25th spot on our list of influential anime symbols. This unbeatable guild boasts formidable generals, with Ainz at the helm. The Nazarick symbol embodies power and the unwavering loyalty of its generals to Ainz.

UA Logo

– Symbol of Aspiring Heroes Anime: My Hero Academia Influence Rating: 59/100

The 24th spot goes to the UA logo from My Hero Academia, initially named Yuuei High School, which means “hero” in Japanese. This logo signifies peace and the relentless efforts of the younger generation striving to become top-notch heroes.

The Crescent Moon

– Empowering Women Anime: Sailor Moon Influence Rating: 60/100

The Crescent Moon symbol from Sailor Moon, a beloved anime from the 90s, comes in at number 2This emblem represents women’s empowerment and intuition. Sailor Moon featured strong female characters in leading roles, promoting women’s empowerment while entertaining audiences worldwide.

 Hime’s Mask

– Princess Mononoke’s Icon Anime: Princess Mononoke Influence Rating: 62/100

At number 22, we have Hime’s Mask from Princess Mononoke, one of the most iconic anime films of all time. Often mistaken for representing God, this mask actually symbolizes the god of forests and spirits. This tribal-looking mask has become a global sensation, frequently seen at cultural festivals in Japan.

SEELE

– Mysterious Eyes of God Anime: Neon Genesis Evangelion Influence Rating: 64/100

For those who’ve watched Neon Genesis Evangelion, the enigmatic organization SEELE holds the 21st spot. Its symbol features seven eyes within a triangle, representing the seven eyes of God, inspired by the biblical supreme deity, Yahweh. Despite being the central antagonist organization, SEELE’s concept resonates with fans.

Kamina’s Great Flaming Skull

– Symbol of Victory Anime: Gurren Lagann Influence Rating: 65/100

Kamina’s Great Flaming Skull, at number 20, became the primary symbol of the popular series Gurren Lagann. This emblem symbolizes victory and the strength of those who fought in a war. It’s a symbol that inspires many to persevere and secure victory, even getting it as a tattoo.

 Saitama Oppai

– A Lighthearted Symbol Anime: One Punch Man Influence Rating: 67/100

At number 19, we have the amusing Saitama Oppai symbol. Saitama, one of the most powerful anime characters, sports this symbol on his hoodie. While it doesn’t hold any deep meaning, it has garnered love from fans worldwide for its quirky and fitting appearance.

Geass

– The Power to Command Anime: Code Geass Influence Rating: 68/100

The 18th spot goes to Geass, a supernatural ability in Code Geass that compels others to obey your commands. Lelouch wields this power, and the symbol, mysterious in its own right, is believed to symbolize a profound revolution, given the transformative nature of Geass.

 Soul Eater Logo

– Strong and Cool Anime: Soul Eater Influence Rating: 70/100

Soul Eater’s logo takes the 17th spot. While the anime’s name, “Soul Eater,” alludes to Asura’s desire to consume innocent souls, the logo represents the series’ protagonist, Soul Evans. This symbol holds influence due to Evans’ strength and cool demeanor, capturing the attention of many.

L – The Genius’s Moniker Anime: Death Note Influence Rating: 71/100

Ranking 16th is “L” from Death Note, often misunderstood as a shortened form of “Lawliet.” However, “L” is pronounced as “El” in Hebrew, signifying “God.” L, a genius with unparalleled intelligence, concealed his identity behind this logo during conferences and important meetings. It symbolized intelligence and brilliance and continued to do so through his successor, Near.

Night Raid Logo

– Symbol of Revolution Anime: Akame ga Kill Influence Rating: 73/100

The Night Raid Logo claims the 15th spot, carrying a profound message. This emblem represents the Night Raid’s fight against the Imperial Army, aiming to bring peace and justice to the capital. For the people, Night Raid symbolized heroism as they opposed the oppressive government, standing up for those in need.

Anime symbols hold a unique place in our hearts, each conveying its own story and meaning. As we explore more influential symbols in the anime world, we continue to uncover the deep connections these symbols forge with fans worldwide.
